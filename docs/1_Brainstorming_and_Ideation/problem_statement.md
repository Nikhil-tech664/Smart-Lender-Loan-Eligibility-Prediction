# Problem Statement

## 1. Context of the Underwriting Domain
In traditional commercial banking and retail lending, credit evaluation is a manual, document-intensive process. Underwriters must inspect files, verify tax statements, cross-examine pay stubs, check credit bureau history guidelines, and manually calculate debt-to-income (DTI) and loan-to-value (LTV) ratios. This process introduces substantial latency, human error, and subjective operational variance.

## 2. Core Problem Rationale
The manual process leads to several institutional bottlenecks:
* **High Operational Latency:** Loan decisions often take 5 to 10 business days to complete, causing applicants to abandon the process in favor of faster digital competitors.
* **Lack of Standardization:** Different human underwriters apply credit guidelines inconsistently, creating compliance risks and subjective bias.
* **Risk Exposure:** Failing to systematically detect correlated risk indicators (e.g., matching low credit history with extreme loan-to-income ratios) leads to higher loan default rates.

## 3. Solution Requirements
To remain competitive, modern financial institutions require an automated, objective, secure, and data-driven risk assessment engine. The solution must process credit telemetry parameters in real time and deliver instantaneous, mathematically sound underwriting recommendations.
